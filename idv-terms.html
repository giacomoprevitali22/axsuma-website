ID Verification Terms

Last updated: 14 April 2026

ID Verification Services

These terms apply specifically to Axsuma's identity verification services provided under the ECSP/ACSP frameworks and applicable legislation. They supplement our Standard Terms of Engagement and govern how we conduct identity verification for clients and their principals.

Identity Verification Services

Axsuma provides identity verification services including:

  • Verification of personal identity against government-issued documents
  • Assessment and verification of beneficial ownership information
  • Validation of company registration and corporate authority
  • Confirmation of directorship and individual authority
  • Preparation of verification reports and certificates
  • Record-keeping and audit trail maintenance
  • Compliance with ECCTA/ACSP verification standards

Our verification services are provided in accordance with applicable legislation, professional standards, and our own quality assurance procedures. We verify information based on documents and information provided by you; we do not conduct independent investigations.

Acceptable Forms of Identification

We accept the following primary forms of identity verification:

  • Passports: Valid passports issued by UK or other national governments
  • Driving Licenses: UK or foreign driving licences with photographic identification
  • National Identity Cards: Government-issued identity cards from EEA and other countries
  • Visas and Travel Documents: Valid visas, residence permits, and official travel documents
  • Official Government Documents: Birth certificates (certified copies), marriage certificates, and other government-issued documents

All documents must be original, certified copies, or officially issued documents. We will not accept photocopies, scanned images without supporting verification, or documents that have been altered or defaced. Documents must be in English or accompanied by certified English translations. Expired documents may be acceptable for certain purposes but will be assessed on a case-by-case basis.

Your Responsibilities

You warrant and agree that:

  • All information and documents provided are accurate, authentic, and not fraudulent
  • You have obtained all necessary consents from individuals whose identity is being verified
  • Personal data will be processed in accordance with GDPR and applicable data protection laws
  • The purpose of identity verification is lawful and not related to financial crime, sanctions evasion, or other prohibited activities
  • You will not attempt to circumvent verification procedures or provide misleading information
  • You understand that false information or forged documents may be reported to authorities

You remain responsible for the accuracy of information provided. If we discover that information has been falsified or misrepresented, we reserve the right to terminate services and may report this to relevant authorities.

Our Verification Process

Our verification process includes:

  • Review of original or certified documents provided
  • Verification of document authenticity (format, security features, issuing authority)
  • Confirmation that identity information matches supplied documentation
  • Assessment of beneficial ownership and control structures
  • Checking against known sanctions lists and adverse media databases
  • Recording all verification steps and results
  • Production of verification reports or certificates upon successful verification

Verification may be rejected if documentation is incomplete, documents cannot be verified, identity information is inconsistent, or individuals fail to cooperate with the verification process. We will advise you of any issues and may provide opportunity to rectify matters.

Data Handling and Record Retention

Personal data collected during identity verification is processed and retained as follows:

  • Processing: Personal data is used only for verification purposes and in accordance with applicable data protection legislation. Data is not used for marketing or sold to third parties.
  • Secure Storage: Documents and personal information are stored securely, either in physical secure locations or encrypted digital systems.
  • Retention Period: Identity verification records are retained for a minimum of 6 years from the date of verification, in accordance with anti-money laundering requirements. Some information may be retained longer where required by law.
  • Access: Access to personal data is limited to Axsuma staff involved in providing services and to regulatory authorities where disclosure is legally required.
  • Disposal: Upon expiry of the retention period, documents and personal data are securely destroyed or anonymised.

You acknowledge that personal data will be processed and that individuals consent to this processing. See our Privacy Policy for full details of data protection rights and procedures.

Verification Certificates and Reports

Upon successful verification, we provide verification certificates or detailed reports confirming:

  • The identity of the individual or entity verified
  • The documents reviewed and verification date
  • The basis of our verification conclusion
  • Any limitations or qualifications to our verification
  • Our reference number for audit and record-keeping purposes

Certificates and reports are prepared for your use and may be disclosed to third parties as necessary. However, these documents remain our intellectual property and must be treated confidentially. You may not modify, misrepresent, or alter our verification certificates or reports.

Fees and Charges

Identity verification fees are set out in our engagement letter or proposal and may be charged on a per-verification basis or as part of a retainer arrangement. Fees are based on the type and complexity of verification required:

  • Individual Verification: Standard fee for verification of a single individual
  • Company Verification: Verification of corporate status and directorship
  • Complex Structures: Verification of beneficial ownership in complex corporate structures (higher fee)
  • Bulk Verifications: Discounted rates for multiple individuals or entities verified simultaneously

Additional charges apply for:

  • Document translation or certified copy preparation
  • Resubmission of failed verifications requiring repeat verification
  • Additional due diligence or adverse media checks
  • Expedited processing or weekend/out-of-hours services

Limitations of Identity Verification

Our identity verification services have inherent limitations:

  • We verify information and documents provided by you; we do not conduct independent investigations or field verification
  • We cannot guarantee the absence of fraud or forgery, particularly where documents have been skilfully altered
  • Verification is based on available information at the time of verification; we are not responsible for information becoming inaccurate subsequently
  • Checks against sanctions and adverse media databases are based on publicly available sources and may not capture all relevant information
  • We do not verify the legitimacy of the purpose for which verification is sought
  • Our verification certificate is evidence of our verification process only; it does not constitute a guarantee of identity or character

You should not rely solely on our verification for purposes where high assurance is required and should consider additional due diligence procedures.

Liability and Professional Indemnity

Our liability for identity verification services is limited to the fees paid in the preceding 12 months or GBP 100,000, whichever is lower. We are not liable for:

  • Fraudulent documents that we fail to detect (unless due to gross negligence)
  • Consequences of verification decisions made by you or third parties based on our verification
  • Information becoming inaccurate following our verification
  • Your failure to follow our recommendations or conduct additional due diligence
  • Regulatory or legal consequences arising from reliance on our verification certificates

We maintain professional indemnity insurance in accordance with regulatory requirements. Details are available on request.

Termination of Services

Either party may terminate identity verification services with 30 days' written notice. Upon termination, we will:

  • Complete any verifications in progress where practicable
  • Provide outstanding verification certificates or reports
  • Retain personal data and verification records in accordance with our retention policy
  • Securely dispose of your instructions upon expiry of retention periods

Termination does not relieve you of obligations to maintain proper identity verification records if required by law.
