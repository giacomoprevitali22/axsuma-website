Identity Verification Services

Companies House authorised identity verification under the Economic Crime and Corporate Transparency Act. Streamlined, expert-guided verification for directors, PSCs, and company members.

From 18 November 2026, identity verification under the UK's Economic Crime and Corporate Transparency Act (ECCTA) became a legal requirement, initiating a 12-month transition period for compliance.

Axsuma is a Companies House Authorised Corporate Service Provider (ACSP), authorised to carry out identity verification on behalf of clients. As part of the UK Government's drive to enhance transparency and strengthen trust in corporate structures, the ECCTA requires all directors, LLP members, Persons with Significant Control (PSCs), and individuals filing on behalf of companies to verify their identity with Companies House. Upon successful verification, each individual will be issued with a unique personal code.

Where a corporate director or a corporate entity is listed as a PSC (i.e. a Relevant Legal Entity), details of at least one director of that entity must also be provided for identity verification. The unique reference code is assigned to the individual rather than their role or any specific company. As such, individuals holding multiple roles (for example, acting as both a director and PSC, or serving across multiple entities) are only required to complete the verification process once.

By choosing to verify through an ACSP, your ID documents and verification evidence are processed and stored by the ACSP rather than within the GOV.UK One Login identity system. This ensures your documentation is managed within a single regulated professional services relationship, rather than a centralised government identity platform.

We offer a streamlined, fully supported process to help you meet these requirements efficiently and with confidence. You can begin the process online, with expert guidance available at every stage.
