UK Address for Service (Intellectual Property)

A designated UK address for intellectual property matters. Reliable receipt and forwarding of IP correspondence and formal notices.

What is an Address for Service?

An address for service is the UK address to which the UK Intellectual Property Office (IPO) or third parties may send correspondence relating to an intellectual property application or registered right, and where documents in IP proceedings may be delivered. It is a statutory requirement for many intellectual property matters and serves as a formal point of contact for IP-related communications.

For overseas companies, overseas individuals, or anyone without a UK address, an address for service provides a UK presence for IP purposes. It ensures that IP-related notices, office action letters, renewal reminders, and legal correspondence are received and properly managed, protecting your intellectual property rights and ensuring compliance with UK IPO deadlines and requirements.

Receipt & Log of IP Correspondence

We receive and log all IP-related notices and correspondence sent to your designated address. This includes communications from the UK Intellectual Property Office, European Patent Office (if applicable), customs authorities, trademark registries, and third parties involved in IP disputes or licensing negotiations.

All correspondence is logged and tracked, ensuring nothing is missed and no important deadlines slip through. We maintain a clear record of what has been received, when it was received, and its current status, giving you complete visibility into your IP-related communications.

Scanning & Secure Forwarding of Documents

Upon receipt, we scan all IP-related documents and securely forward them to your nominated contact or legal advisers. Documents are scanned and delivered via secure email, ensuring you have immediate visibility of all correspondence without delay. Original physical documents can be retained for your records if required, or securely destroyed following your instructions.

The scanning and forwarding process is rapid—typically completed within one business day of receipt. This ensures that time-sensitive IP matters (such as responses to office actions, oppositions, or enforcement proceedings) can be addressed without unnecessary delay.

Documented Confirmation of Receipt

Where required for legal proceedings or regulatory purposes, we provide documented confirmation of receipt for IP correspondence. This is particularly important in disputes, infringement matters, or situations where evidence of timely receipt is material to your legal position.

Formal receipt certificates can be provided for court proceedings or administrative proceedings, establishing that correspondence was received on a specific date. This protects your legal interests and ensures you can evidence compliance with statutory deadlines and requirements.

Coordination with Your Legal Advisers

We coordinate closely with your in-house legal team, external IP counsel, or enforcement advisers. Where correspondence requires immediate legal attention (such as office actions, oppositions, or infringement notices), we can flag these to your nominated advisers for expedited response.

We can provide context around incoming correspondence, assist with deadline tracking, and facilitate efficient handoff of matters to your legal advisers. For overseas companies with limited UK presence, this coordination service is invaluable—it ensures your IP matters receive the timely professional attention they require.
