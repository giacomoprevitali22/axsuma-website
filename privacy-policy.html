Privacy Policy

Last updated: 14 April 2026

Our Commitment to Your Privacy

Axsuma Ltd is committed to protecting your personal data and respecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, your rights under the UK General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), and how to contact us with questions about your data.

Information We Collect

Axsuma collects personal data in various ways to provide our corporate services effectively:

  • Information You Provide: When you contact us, complete forms, or engage our services, we collect your name, email address, phone number, job title, company details, and any other information you share with us.
  • Service-Related Data: As a corporate services provider, we may collect or process sensitive business information, company registration details, shareholder information, beneficial ownership data, and identification documents to fulfil our legal obligations.
  • Website Interaction Data: We collect information about how you interact with our website, including pages visited, time spent on pages, links clicked, IP addresses, browser type, and device information through cookies and analytics tools.
  • Communications Data: We retain copies of emails, messages, and notes from your communications with us to maintain accurate service records and comply with regulatory requirements.

How We Use Your Personal Data

We process your personal data for the following purposes:

  • Service Delivery: To provide corporate services including company formations, governance support, M&A transaction management, accounting services, process agent services, Register of Overseas Entities compliance, and ID verification.
  • Legal and Regulatory Compliance: To comply with UK company law, GDPR, register filing requirements, anti-money laundering regulations (AML), and other applicable legal obligations.
  • Client Communication: To respond to inquiries, provide updates about services, send invoices and payment reminders, and maintain ongoing client relationships.
  • Website Improvement: To understand user behaviour, optimise website performance, and enhance your experience with our online services.
  • Business Administration: For internal accounting, auditing, quality assurance, and management of our business operations.
  • Marketing (With Consent): With your explicit consent, we may send information about new services, events, or insights relevant to your business needs.

Legal Basis for Processing

Under GDPR, we process your personal data on the following legal bases:

  • Contract Performance: Processing necessary to fulfil our service agreement with you and perform our contractual obligations.
  • Legal Obligation: Processing required by law, including UK company law, GDPR, AML regulations, and Companies House filing requirements.
  • Legitimate Interests: Processing in our legitimate business interests, such as improving our services, preventing fraud, and maintaining security, balanced against your rights.
  • Consent: For marketing communications and optional analytics, we rely on your explicit, freely-given consent, which you may withdraw at any time.

Sharing Your Data

Your personal data is treated as confidential and is only shared when necessary to provide our services or comply with legal obligations:

  • Service Partners: We may share data with third-party service providers (such as software platforms, payment processors, and regulatory filing services) who assist us in delivering services. All partners are bound by confidentiality agreements.
  • Regulatory Bodies: We are required to share certain information with Companies House, HMRC, Financial Conduct Authority, and other regulatory authorities as mandated by law.
  • Public Registers: Information filed at Companies House or other public registers becomes publicly available as required by law.
  • Business Transfers: In the unlikely event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of our business, your data may be transferred as part of that transaction, and we would notify you of any such change.
  • Legal Requirements: We may disclose data if required by law, court order, or legitimate law enforcement requests.

We do not sell or rent your personal data to third parties for their own marketing purposes.

Your Data Protection Rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right of Access: You have the right to request a copy of all personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to Rectification: You can request that we correct inaccurate or incomplete personal data.
  • Right to Erasure: You may request deletion of your data, subject to our legal retention obligations and ongoing service requirements.
  • Right to Restrict Processing: You can ask us to limit how we process your data in certain circumstances.
  • Right to Data Portability: You have the right to obtain your data in a structured, commonly-used, machine-readable format and have it transferred to another organisation.
  • Right to Object: You can object to our processing of your data for certain purposes, particularly marketing.
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Where we rely on your consent, you may withdraw it at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.
  • Right to Lodge a Complaint: If you believe your rights have been violated, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO).

How Long We Keep Your Data

We retain your personal data for as long as necessary to provide our services and comply with legal obligations:

  • Service Data: Personal information related to services is retained for the duration of our engagement and for a minimum of 6 years after completion, in compliance with UK company law and tax requirements.
  • Regulatory Records: Data required by Companies House, HMRC, or other regulatory bodies is retained for the periods mandated by law.
  • Marketing Contacts: If you have consented to receive marketing, we retain your contact details until you unsubscribe.
  • Website Analytics: Anonymous analytics data is typically retained for up to 26 months.

Once the retention period expires and there is no legal reason to keep your data, we securely delete or anonymise it.

Data Security

Axsuma takes data security seriously and implements appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorised access, alteration, or loss:

  • Encrypted data transmission (SSL/TLS) for all online services
  • Secure password policies and access controls
  • Regular security assessments and compliance monitoring
  • Staff training on data protection and confidentiality
  • Secure physical storage of documents and records

While we implement robust security measures, no system is completely immune to security breaches. We will notify you promptly if a breach occurs that affects your data.

Cookies and Tracking Technologies

Our website uses cookies and similar tracking technologies. For detailed information about the types of cookies we use and how to manage your preferences, please refer to our Cookie Policy.
