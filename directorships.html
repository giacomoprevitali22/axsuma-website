Professional Directorship Services

Independent, experienced directors for SPVs, holding companies, and family offices. Governance credibility where it matters.

Independent Board Oversight for Complex Structures

In many acquisitions, private equity investments, and complex corporate structures, professional directors bring essential governance credibility and independent oversight. Companies require directors—not just corporate secretaries—to ensure statutory compliance, manage conflicts of interest, and provide fiduciary duty oversight. We provide professional directors (both executive and non-executive) who join company boards to deliver the governance discipline that investors, lenders, and stakeholders expect.

This is particularly valuable in management buyouts, institutional investments, multi-tier holding structures, or where management team members have competing interests. A professional director—independent, experienced, and accountable—ensures the board makes informed decisions with proper oversight, that statutory obligations are met, and that the company maintains governance credibility through transition periods and ongoing operations.

Independent & Non-Executive Directors

Professional directors ensure the company has independent board scrutiny of major decisions and compliance with the Companies Act 2006 director duties (care, diligence, good faith, independent judgment, and avoiding conflicts). Our professional directors have extensive experience in M&A transactions, post-acquisition integration, financial restructuring, and corporate governance across sectors and deal sizes.

As non-executive or independent directors, they bring objectivity to board decision-making, challenge management assumptions, ensure proper financial controls, and provide investor confidence. In acquisition scenarios, they serve as the neutral party overseeing completion mechanics, managing earnout determinations, and ensuring post-completion integration remains compliant. For holding companies and fund structures, they provide the governance oversight that limited partners and institutional investors require.

Professional directors also manage conflicts of interest that arise in complex transactions. Where selling shareholders roll equity into the buyer, where management teams have competing interests, or where institutional investors have board representation, professional directors manage conflict disclosure procedures and ensure decisions are made with proper independent oversight.

SPV & Acquisition Vehicle Directors

Special Purpose Vehicles (SPVs) and acquisition vehicles (BidCos) require careful governance to ensure the structure delivers its intended purpose and maintains corporate separation from the parent. We provide directors specifically for SPV and acquisition structures, ensuring these entities maintain independent board governance, proper record-keeping, and statutory compliance.

SPV directors oversee the execution of the specific transaction or function the SPV is established for—whether acquisition of assets, segregation of operational risk, or holding of specific business units. They ensure the SPV maintains arm's length transfer pricing with parent company entities, that intercompany agreements are properly documented, and that the SPV's separate corporate existence is preserved through proper capitalisation, board governance, and statutory filing.

For acquisition vehicles in multi-tier structures (HoldCo/MidCo/BidCo), directors manage the execution of the acquisition, completion mechanics, working capital adjustments, and post-completion integration. They oversee earnout satisfaction determinations, manage any seller indemnity claims, and ensure the acquired business is properly integrated into the wider group governance framework.

Holding Company & Fund Governance Directors

Holding companies managing multiple subsidiary companies and fund structures require directors who understand multi-entity governance, consolidated group reporting, and investor relations. Professional directors for holding companies manage group-wide strategic decisions, oversee subsidiary performance and integration, and ensure the group structure operates with proper governance disciplines.

For funds and family offices, directors provide the governance credibility that limited partners and institutional investors expect. They oversee investment decisions, monitor performance against fund objectives, manage distribution mechanics, and ensure compliance with fund documentation and investment guidelines. Where fund structures involve multiple tiers (GP companies, GP-led secondaries, or continuation vehicles), professional directors manage governance across the structure.

Holding company directors also facilitate communication between parent company boards and subsidiary management teams, oversee dividend distribution mechanics, manage cash flow coordination across the group, and ensure consolidated group reporting is accurate and timely. In family office structures, they provide the independent governance that protects family assets and ensures long-term stewardship.

Resident & Corporate Directors

Companies are required to have at least one director who is a natural person resident in the UK. Foreign entities establishing UK operations, overseas-controlled SPVs, and private equity firms establishing acquisition structures often require local, resident directors to satisfy this statutory requirement. We provide qualified resident directors who maintain a UK residential address complying with Companies House requirements.

Corporate directors offer an additional governance option. Rather than appointing an individual director, a company can appoint a corporate director (itself a company). This is useful where directorship responsibility needs to be segregated within a group structure or where a sponsor company wants to maintain governance control without individual director appointment. We establish and manage corporate director entities on behalf of clients.

Both resident and corporate director appointments involve proper statutory notification to Companies House, including director appointment forms (Form AP01), residential address filings, and maintenance of statutory registers. We manage all director appointment procedures, ensuring regulatory compliance and documentation accuracy.

Director Administration & Statutory Compliance

Beyond board governance, professional directors require comprehensive administrative support to discharge their statutory duties. We manage all director-related compliance: director appointment and resignation procedures, preparation of board meeting minutes reflecting statutory decision-making, management of director appointment forms (Form AP01) and resignation forms (Form TM01), and maintenance of statutory registers reflecting current director details and addresses.

Directors are required to file residential address information with Companies House, with the option to maintain a private register for residential addresses. We manage director address confidentiality filing, ensuring sensitive residential information is protected while remaining compliant with statutory requirements. When directors change (appointments, resignations, retirements), we coordinate all notification procedures and statutory filings.

We also coordinate directors' disqualification checks, conflict of interest management, director indemnity insurance arrangements, and attendance at board meetings. Our professional directors provide detailed board minutes documenting decisions, authorisations, and governance actions, ensuring the company maintains a clear record of board governance and statutory compliance.
