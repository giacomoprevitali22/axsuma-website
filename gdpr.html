GDPR & Data Protection Statement

Last updated: 14 April 2026

Our GDPR Compliance Commitment

Axsuma is committed to compliance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and Data Protection Act 2018. This statement summarises how we protect personal data, your rights as a data subject, and how to contact us regarding data protection matters.

What is GDPR?

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is a comprehensive EU legal framework on data protection that applies in the UK. GDPR sets out strict rules for how organisations must handle personal data and provides individuals with significant rights to control their information. Axsuma processes personal data in accordance with GDPR at all times.

GDPR applies to the processing of personal data of individuals (data subjects) regardless of whether the processing occurs in the UK, EU, or elsewhere. Organisations handling UK or EU residents' data are required to comply with GDPR or face significant penalties.

Your Data Subject Rights Under GDPR

GDPR provides individuals with the following rights regarding their personal data:

  • Right of Access (Article 15): You have the right to request and receive a copy of all personal data Axsuma holds about you, including how it is used and who it is shared with.
  • Right to Rectification (Article 16): If personal data held about you is inaccurate or incomplete, you have the right to request correction or completion.
  • Right to Erasure (Article 17): Under certain circumstances, you have the right to request that we delete your personal data. This does not apply where we have a legal obligation to retain data.
  • Right to Restrict Processing (Article 18): You can request that we limit how we process your data, such as restricting to storage only while we verify disputed information.
  • Right to Data Portability (Article 20): You have the right to obtain your personal data in a structured, commonly-used format and have it transferred to another organisation.
  • Right to Object (Article 21): You have the right to object to processing of your data for certain purposes, particularly direct marketing.
  • Rights Related to Automated Decision-Making (Article 22): You have rights regarding decisions made solely through automated processing affecting you.
  • Right to Withdraw Consent (Article 7): Where processing is based on your consent, you have the right to withdraw consent at any time.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact our Data Protection Officer. We will verify your identity and respond to requests within 30 days (or 90 days for complex requests).

Lawful Basis for Processing

GDPR requires that all personal data processing is based on one of six lawful bases. Axsuma processes personal data on the following bases:

  • Contract Performance (Article 6(1)(b)): Processing necessary to perform our contractual obligations to you as a client.
  • Legal Obligation (Article 6(1)(c)): Processing required by UK law, company law, taxation law, anti-money laundering regulations, and regulatory requirements.
  • Vital Interests (Article 6(1)(d)): Processing necessary to protect vital interests (rare, such as health/safety emergencies).
  • Public Task (Article 6(1)(e)): Processing necessary for a task in the public interest or official authority (limited application for Axsuma).
  • Legitimate Interests (Article 6(1)(f)): Processing pursued where our legitimate business interests outweigh your rights. These include improving services, preventing fraud, maintaining security, and client administration.
  • Consent (Article 6(1)(a)): Processing based on your explicit, freely-given, informed consent (primarily for marketing communications).

Sensitive Data and Special Categories

GDPR recognises that certain categories of personal data require additional protection (Article 9 special categories). These include data relating to racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious beliefs, trade union membership, genetic data, biometric data, health data, and data about criminal convictions and offences.

Axsuma processes special category data only where:

  • Processing is necessary to comply with legal obligations (such as beneficial ownership or sanction check requirements)
  • Processing is necessary for the exercise or defence of legal claims
  • You have explicitly consented to processing
  • Processing is necessary for employment or social protection purposes

We apply strict safeguards to special category data, including enhanced access controls, encryption, and confidentiality measures.

International Data Transfers

GDPR restricts transfer of personal data outside the UK/EEA to countries with an adequate level of data protection. Axsuma operates in the UK and processes most personal data within the UK. However, where international transfers are necessary:

  • We only transfer data to countries recognised by the UK Government or having adequate safeguards
  • We use standard contractual clauses (SCCs) to ensure appropriate data protection for transfers outside adequate jurisdictions
  • We obtain your explicit consent for transfers where required
  • We implement additional technical and organisational measures to protect transferred data

If you have concerns about international data transfers, please contact our Data Protection Officer for details of the safeguards applied.

Data Breach Notification and Response

Axsuma maintains procedures to detect, assess, and respond to personal data breaches. In the event of a breach that poses a risk to individuals' rights and freedoms:

  • We will notify the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) within 72 hours of discovery
  • We will notify affected individuals without undue delay where there is a high risk to their rights
  • We will provide information about the breach, our response, and steps individuals can take to protect themselves
  • We will conduct a thorough investigation and implement additional safeguards to prevent recurrence

If you believe we have experienced a data breach, please contact our Data Protection Officer immediately at dpo@axsuma.co.uk.

Privacy by Design and Data Protection Impact Assessments

Axsuma is committed to Privacy by Design principles, which means data protection is incorporated into all aspects of our operations:

  • Systems and processes are designed with data protection as a core consideration, not an afterthought
  • We minimise collection and processing of personal data (data minimisation)
  • We limit retention of personal data to periods necessary for legal or business purposes
  • We implement default privacy protections that protect individuals automatically
  • We conduct Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) for high-risk processing

GDPR Training and Staff Accountability

All Axsuma staff involved in processing personal data receive training on GDPR and our data protection procedures. Staff are required to:

  • Understand GDPR principles and their obligations
  • Follow our Data Protection Policy and information security procedures
  • Treat personal data confidentially and securely
  • Report any suspected data protection breaches or misuse of data
  • Undergo regular refresher training on data protection requirements

Axsuma maintains records of staff training and compliance to demonstrate accountability (GDPR Article 5 accountability principle).
