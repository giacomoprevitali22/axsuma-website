Complete UK Company Formation Solutions

Axsuma provides a complete company formation solution, whether you are seeking a straightforward private limited company incorporation or need additional support for a bespoke entity or complex SPV. We guide you through every stage of formation—from name availability verification through to post-incorporation and statutory compliance.

We specialise in navigating the Companies House registration process, managing articles of association, establishing company governance, and ensuring you meet all statutory obligations from incorporation onwards. Our team has processed hundreds of incorporations, from straightforward single-director companies to complex multi-shareholder corporate structures.

Private Limited Companies

A private company limited by shares is the most common business structure in the UK, offering its owners limited liability protection. UK private limited companies are a popular vehicle for operational businesses as well as being commonly used as Holding Companies or SPVs for specific transactions.

Axsuma can create a new UK Private Limited Company with their standard Articles of Association, the Model Articles or can use bespoke Articles provided by clients.

Ready-Made Company Stock

Axsuma holds a stock of UK ready-made private limited companies that can be made available and reserved immediately, ideal for urgent transactions, such as time-sensitive acquisitions. You acquire the company through a transfer of shares, change directors and shareholders through standard resolutions.

We hold a stock of single stand-alone entities or stacks, comprising of Hold Co, Mid Co and Bid Co specifically for use with highly confidential Merger and Acquisition transactions that can be reserved on a "held to order" basis.

Limited Liability Partnerships (LLPs)

The UK Limited Liability Partnership (LLP) provides a UK vehicle that has the tax status and flexibility of a partnership, but with a separate legal personality and limited liability for its members. Unlike traditional partnerships, LLP members enjoy limited liability protection—you're not personally liable for the negligence or misconduct of other members.

LLP formation mirrors company incorporation but with distinct statutory requirements. All new UK LLPs will need to operate under the provisions set out in the LLP Regulations (Regulation 7), however, Axsuma recommends that a specific LLP operating agreement is adopted. The LLP agreement defines member contributions, profit-sharing arrangements, management rights, member exit procedures, and dispute resolution.
